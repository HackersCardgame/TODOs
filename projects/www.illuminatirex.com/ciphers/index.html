Illuminati Novice Cipher


Simple substitution cipher used by Illuminati Novice and Minervals. The numbers are from Ammy’s Handwriting.

Early 20th century Illuminati researcher René Le Forestier notes in his Les Illumines de Bavière et la Franc-Maçonnerie Allemande that the Bavarian Illuminati used this cipher most frequently because of its simplicity. (p. 53-54) They were many different versions of the glyph cipher used by Scottish Knights floating around and few members were able to memorize the keys.

The simple cipher was rarely used to write entire documents, but rather to hide the profane names and occupations of members.

The Scottish Knight cipher eventually fell into disuse by Illuminati members. The reasons given were that they were too many versions of the cipher floating around and cipher keys had become inconsistent. Members frequently made use of the simple cipher instead, but then usually only to obscure names and places.
